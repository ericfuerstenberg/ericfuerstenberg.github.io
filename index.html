Age-related patterns of cognitive performance · Master's Thesis · September 2015

For my Master's thesis I employed generalized additive modeling and piecewise regression to construct a model of cognitive development and aging across the human lifespan. As a part of this project I also leveraged an extensive dataset on household production to assess the social and economic returns to investment in education in developing populations.

Google analytics location history · Personal project · August 2015


If you have an Android device Google knows where you are. It's a bit unsettling but the resulting dataset is mind blowing. This project was simple; I used the Google Location API to analyze how my daily patterns have changed throughout 4 years of life in Santa Barbara.


Life-history model of cognitive aging · Personal project

As people age things slow down. Walking speed, mental sharpness, reaction times all follow a similar pattern decline. But is it all downhill after 40? Working with psychologists and anthropologists and conducting fieldwork among hunter-horticulturalists in Bolivia, I've been involved in a study documenting changes in cognitive performance throughout late life - and our results suggest that the picture isn't all bad.


Trends in GRE scores and enrollments by gender · Intelligence · November 2014



Over the past 30 years Women have made considerable progress toward equitable representation in higher education - with female students typically making up a larger proportion of university students than males. However, in graduate programs (and particularly in the hard sciences and engineering fields) women are still at a disadvantage. In this project we utilized 30+ years of ETS data to analyze yearly changes in GRE scores and eventual placement in graduate programs by college major. Results were published in Intelligence.
